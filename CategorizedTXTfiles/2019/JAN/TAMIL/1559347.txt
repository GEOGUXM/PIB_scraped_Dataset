      “800 Folk art programmes in TN to protect and educate girl Children”
      Posted On: 10 JAN 2019 2:49PM by PIB Chennai


    A One-day workshop to orient Folk Artists to successfully propagate Government of India's 'Beti Bachao Beti Padhao' scheme was organised by Regional Outreach Bureau, Ministry of Information and Broadcasting in Chennai today.


    The workshop was organised in collaboration with Ministry of Women & Child Development, Govt of India, and Department of Social Welfare, Government of Tamil Nadu.


    The importance of folk artists in creating awareness among the people on protecting a Girl child and educating her was emphasized in the sessions.


    Inaugurating the event, Mrs. V. Amuthavalli, Commissioner of Social Welfare, Government of Tamil Nadu said, the government is successfully implementing several programmes to protect, nourish and educate girl children from their birth. The commissioner urged the folk artist troops to effectively take the message across the state in implementing the scheme.


    Over 800 programs in next two months have been planned across the state to take the message of 'Beti Bachao Beti Padhao' through folk artists.


    Joint Director Mrs B. Sundari, Department of Social Welfare spoke and gave guidelines in dissemination of the message.


    Officials from Regional Outreach Bureau, and PIB participated in the programme.
