      Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas

      E-MPUY 2 launched to achieve 100 per cent LPG penetration. Free LPG connections under PMUY touch 6 crore mark.
      Posted On: 03 JAN 2019 3:18PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, January, 03, 2019.


    With the launch of the Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) scheme, LPG penetration in West Bengal has increased from 40.5 per cent in August 2014 to 88.1per cent in December 2018. The number of LPG customers has also gone up to 195.75 lakhs in December, 2018 against 86.64 lakhs in August, 2014, informed Shri Abhijit Dey, Chief General Manager, (LPG), Indian Oil Corporation Ltd. here today. Interacting with the media persons Shri Dey informed that 6 crore free LPG connections so far have been distributed under the PMUY scheme. PMUY, a scheme to provide 5 crore deposit-free LPG connections to women belonging to the Below Poverty Line (BPL) families in India, has been subsequently increased to cover 8 crore with a budgetary allocation of Rs 12,800 crore.


    Shri Dey stated that the Extended Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana-2 (E-PMUY 2) scheme has been approved by the Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA). He said, under this scheme, BPL households not covered under the Socio-Economic Cast Census (SECC) data and EPMUY would be facilitated to avail of the PMUY LPG connection by showing their Ration Cards. This scheme will lead to near 100 per cent LPG penetration in the state, he added.


    To create awareness among rural households and expand LPG coverage further, 2778 LPG Panachayats out of 14 thousand, have been conducted so far throughout the state to educate users on safe use and benefits of LPG, he informed.


    Several initiatives including home delivery by Grameen Distributorships have been taken to provide hassle free services to the consumers. In case of emergency, one can call on 1906 emergency services. For queries and help related to PMUY, one can call on Toll Free No. 18002333555.


    Shri Amitava Majumdar, Chief General Manager, I/C, West Bengal, IOCL and Smt. Maria Bhattacharya, General Manager (LPG) were also present on the occasion.
