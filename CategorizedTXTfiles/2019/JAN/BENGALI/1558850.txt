      Ministry of Textiles

      “Artisan Speak & Jute Expo” – a textile outreach initiative starts from today Union Ministry of Textiles to take initiative for production of low cost sanitary napkins: Union Textiles Secretary
      Posted On: 07 JAN 2019 2:29PM by PIB Kolkata


    -Kolkata, 7 January, 2019


    A textiles outreach initiative of the Ministry of Textiles named “Artisan Speak & Jute Expo” is being held in the newly restored heritage structure of Old Currency Building here in the city today. The programme to be inaugurated by Union Minister of Textiles Smt. Smriti Zubin Irani today will continue till January 10, 2019.


    Shri Raghavendra Singh, Secretary, Union Ministry of Textiles lighted the lamp in the programme which started today morning. On the occasion, the Textiles Secretary said, this event mainly aims to promote Indian handloom, handicraft, silk and jute and provide platform for marketing and business linkages with international buyers and also the domestic market. This initiative will also open up new opportunities for artisans for exhibiting their products for four days. The objective of this Outreach Textile Event is to project and create awareness about the textile industry by inviting international buyers, agents and other marketing organizations, establish B2B connects, to provide knowledge with design intervention for viable marketing for improving exports and domestic presence, as stated by the Secretary.


    Speaking of an unique initiative being taken by the Ministry of Textiles in collaboration with Ministry of Women and Child Development and a few other stakeholder ministries, Shri Raghavendra Singh also stated, cost-effective sanitary napkins made of jute will be produced to be supplied to women all over the country to address their hygienic needs at a low cost.
