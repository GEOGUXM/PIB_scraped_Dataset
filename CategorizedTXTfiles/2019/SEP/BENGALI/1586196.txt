      BUST OF PANDIT ISWARCHANDRA VIDYASAGAR UNVEILED AT THE ASIATIC SOCIETY
      Posted On: 25 SEP 2019 6:48PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, September 25, 2019


    A bust of Pandit Iswarchandra Vidyasagar was unveiled and an exhibition entitled Iswarchandra Vidyasagar as Printer and Publisher was inaugurated at Vidyasagar Hall of the Asiatic Society in Kolkata today.


    Professor Sukanta Chaudhuri, Professor Emeritus, Department of English, Jadavpur University delivered a lecture on Iswarchandra Vidyasagarâ€™s Contribution to Scholarship, Society and Culture.In his address, Professor Chaudhuri said that Pandit Iswarchandra Vidyasagar was a well-known writer, philosopher and a staunch supporter of the humanity. Revered by the British authorities of his time, Vidyasagar brought about a revolution in the Bengali education system and refined the way Bengali language was written and taught. Vidyasagar is credited for championing the cause of education, especially for girls, he added and informed that Vidyasagar founded several schools for girls in the early 19th century. He strongly believed that everyone, irrespective of their caste or gender, had the right to education and so he opened up the premises of the Sanskrit College for people from lower castes.


    Pandit Iswarchandra also reconstructed the Bengali alphabet and reformed Bengali typography into an alphabet of 12 vowels and 40 consonants.He authored many books which greatly helped the Bengali education system.


    Professor Isha Mahammad, president of the Asiatic Society and Shri S.B. Chakrabarti, General Secretary, the Asiatic Society among others were also present on the occasion.
