      Balmer Lawrie and Co.Ltd is constantly innovating and leveraging its strengths to be a future ready company, says C&MD.
      Posted On: 18 SEP 2019 7:47PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, September 18, 2018.


    Balmer Lawrie and Company Ltd. has recorded net turnover of Rs. 1,857 crore during 2018-19 as against Rs.1,796 crore in 2017-18 registering an increase of approximately 3%. The company has as well recorded a profit before tax of R. 280.10 crore in 2018-19 as against Rs. 261.11 crore in 2017-18.This was stated by Shri Prabal Basu, Chairman and Managing Director (C&MD)Balmer Lawrie & Company at its 10251 annual general meeting held in the city today. Shri Basu said that the increase is attributed to improved performance of various strategic business units(SBUs), Travel, Vacations and higher dividend income earned during 2018-19.


    Shri Basu stated that the company has completed 152 years of its existence and still becoming stronger. It has survived many challenges, reinvented itself and adapted to various changes in order to compete with the market. It lever aged every change as an opportunity and remained profitable by strengthening core businesses, for aying into new businesses and exiting those, which were not doing well, he added.


    Calling it the diversified PSU, Shri Basu said that industrial packaging unit (IPU) has managed to increase its volume over the last few years. A new manufacturing facility, set up at Vadodara, Gujarat, is providing a significant edge to the SBU for further growth. He Balmerol brand has been recognized as one of India's best brands in 2019 by The Economic Times. Refinery and Oil Field Services is the pioneer in mechanized oily sludge processing in India and maintains leadership position with around 70% market share, Shri Basu informed and added that with patented technology back up, the SBU could process a substantial amount of oily sludgeduringthepast twodecadesand have developedaloyallistof satisfiedclientele. To maintain its competitive edge in the market, the SBU is focused on technology upgradation to stay at par with global standards, Shri Basu added.
