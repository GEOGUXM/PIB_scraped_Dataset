      Ministry of Micro,Small & Medium Enterprises

      Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA)
      
      Workshop on National Pension System (NPS) for Corporates

      Posted On: 19 NOV 2019 3:09PM by PIB Mumbai

        Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) conducted a workshop on National Pension System (NPS) for Corporates in Mumbai yesterday. The workshop was organized in association of Maharashtra FICCI. The workshop was informed that the overall number of NPS and APY subscribers in the country has crossed 3.12 crores with Asset under Management (AUM) of more than 3.85 lakh crores as on 9th November 2019.


        Mrs. Sucheta Shah, Chairperson, FICCI Maharashtra MSME Committee in her welcome address lauded the efforts of PFRDA for organizing such workshops for creating awareness about NPS and apprised the corporates of retirement benefits options that are available for adoption for their employees.


        Speaking on the occasion Supratim Bandyopadhyay, Whole Time Member, PFRDA said that with the significant implementation of pension reforms, India’s ranking in the ‘ Melbourne Mercer Global Pension Index 2019 ’ report has improved marginally to 32 nd position. Government impetus of tax incentives on NPS contributions and lump sum withdrawals on exit from NPS are making NPS effective and the impact is evident in the non-government sector as NPS enrollments has registered a growth of 60% as compared to last financial year. A total of 6870 corporates has already adopted NPS for its employees and 20 lacs subscribers are enrolled in the non-government sector as on 9 th November 2019.


        More than 160 participants from around 90 corporates along with the registered PoPs and Pension Funds attended the workshop.
