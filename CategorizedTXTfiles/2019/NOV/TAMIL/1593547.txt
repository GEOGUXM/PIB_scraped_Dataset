      6.8 Kgs of Gold, worth Rs. 2.28 crores seized by Customs at Chennai Airport
      Posted On: 26 NOV 2019 2:56PM by PIB Chennai


    On 24.11.2019, on specific information that huge quantity of gold is being smuggled from Colombo by one Mohamed Niyas in the Sri Lankan airlines flight UL125 /24.11.2019, Customs officials maintained a special watch at aerobridge , tarmac, arrival hall area of Anna International Airport and intercepted Mohamed Niyas of S P Pattinam, arrived by UL 125 who traveled from Dubai as transit passenger at Colombo and admitted to have distributed gold in paste form to the 29 passengers. The said 29 passengers were also intercepted and accepted to have received gold from Niyas. Around 7.5 kg of gold in paste form concealed in their rectum was recovered. On extraction 24 kt gold weighing 6.4 kg worth Rs 2.16 crore ( International value ) was recovered and seized under the provisions of the Customs Act, 1962. One of the 29 passengers was arrested based upon his previous offence .


    On the same night, Customs officials on suspicion , intercepted one male pax Aqueab Hussain of Kadapa who arrived from Kuwait by Kuwait airways flight KU343/24.11.2019 . On search of his hand baggage gold sheets, gold rings, cut bits concealed inside two wrist watches were recovered. The recovered gold totally weighing 352 gms worth Rs11,88,000/- was seized under the Customs Act 1962.


    Further Investigation is under progress according to a press release issued by the Commissioner of Customs, Chennai International Airport, Chennai.
