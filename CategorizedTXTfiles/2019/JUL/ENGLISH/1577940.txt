    DPE has issued Guidelines dated 29.10.2015 for â€œStreamlining the mechanism for revival and restructuring of sick/ incipient sick and weak Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs)". As per these guidelines, the CPSEs having negative net worth are considered as sick. As per information available with DPE, there were 52,45, 48, 54 and 56 CPSEs having negative net worth as on 31.03.2014, 31.03.2015, 31.03.2016, 31.03.2017 and 31.03.2018 respectively. The details of CPSEs with negative net worth as on 31.03.2018 are given at Annex-II.


    Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs) play a major role in fulfilling developmental priorities of the country. They contribute to macroeconomic objectives such as growth, advancement in manufacturing and technology and price stabilization, as well as to fulfill socio-economic objectives such as employment generation and skill development.


    CPSEs function under the control of administrative ministries. The decisions relating to investments in the CPSEs are taken by the concerned administrative ministries with the approval of competent authority considering techno-economic feasibility.
