      Ministry of Women and Child Development

      Modernisation Programme for Anganwadi Centres
      Posted On: 19 JUL 2019 5:19PM by PIB Delhi


    Providing modern and essential facilities to cater the needs of women and children at the Anganwadi Centres (AWCs) is a continuing process. A number of steps in this direction have been undertaken in all States including Punjab and Bihar, some of which are as under:


    Revised joint guidelines have been issued by Ministries of Women & Child Development, Rural Development and Panchayati Raj on 17.02.2016 for construction of 4 Lakhs AWC buildings across the country under Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS) in convergence with Anganwadi Services (ICDS Scheme). The State-wise statement is at Annexure-I.


    Drinking water facilities have been provided in 87.4% Anganwadi Centres.


    Sanitation facilities have been provided in 71.98% Anganwadi Centres.


    Grants are sanctioned for purchase of water filter for serving safe drinking water, furniture, equipments, etc.


    Swachhta Action Plan â€“ Rs. 12,000/-per AWC are given for providing drinking water facilities and Rs.10,000/- per AWC for providing toilet facilities.


    As on 30.06.2019, 3.54 lakh Anganwadi Workers (AWWs) have been provided with Smart Phones for IT-enabled monitoring under ICDS-Common Application Software (CAS). State/UT-wise statement is at Annexure-II .


    Under POSHAN Abhiyaan 11,51,517 weighing machines and 5,37,187 Stadiometer have been provided for growth monitoring.


    Funds for ECCE have been enhanced to Rs 5,000 per AWC. Activity books have been introduced in the Scheme.


    For nutritional deficiency, norms prescribed in Schedule-II of NFSA, 2013, are followed:

    A Memorandum of Understanding has been signed between Ministry of Women & Child Development and M/s Vedanta on 21st September, 2015 for construction of 4,000 Anganwadi Centres by M/s Vedanta, through its own resources, as part of their Corporate Social Responsibilities primarily in the States of Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Odisha, Rajasthan, Telangana and Uttar Pradesh.


    M/s. Vedanta has already started construction of Anganwadi buildings in Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh. As per monthly progress report submitted by Vedanta, construction of 552 AWC buildings (5 AWC buildings in Madhya Pradesh; 499 AWC buildings in Rajasthan; 48 AWC buildings in Uttar Pradesh) has already been completed by 30.04.2019.
