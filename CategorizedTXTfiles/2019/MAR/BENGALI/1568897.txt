      Ministry of Culture

      Two-day International Seminar on Influence of Swami Vivekananda and Guatam Buddha on Culture and Civilization of South East Asia inaugurated at MAKAIAS
      Posted On: 15 MAR 2019 3:38PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, March 15, 2019


    A two-day International Seminar on “Influence of Swami Vivekananda and Guatam Buddha on Culture and Civilization of South East Asia” organized by Maulana Abul Kalam Azad Institute of Asian Studies (MAKAIAS) was inaugurated here in Kolkata today.


    Speaking at the inaugural session, Shri Ganbold. G, Ambassador of Mongolia in India stated that forums like this help us exchange views and strengthen our cultural and spiritual ties. The historical connection between India and Mongolia is for over 2500 years. There were historical, cultural and religious connections and beside these, now, there is economic, commercial and educational relationship between both the countries, he informed and added that Gautam Buddha’s teachings have immensely influenced lives of Mongolian people.


    Dr. M. Medhankara Thero from Maha Bodhi Society of India and Swami Kamalasthnanda, Principal, RKMVC College, Rahara, Kolkata, stated that Swamin Vivekananda as a spiritual leader of Hinduism had very deep veneration to Lord Buddha. Both believed in the theory of non-violence, karma vada, moral principles and the theory of liberation.


    Scholars from Vietnam, Mongolia, Malaysia, Thailand, Sri Lanka, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Philippines, Myanmar along with scholars from India participated in the seminar.


    Dr. Srikant Mohapatra, Vice-Chancellor, Odisha State Open University; Professor K.C.Baral from English and Foreign Languages University, Hyderabad; Dr. Sujit K. Ghosh, Chairman, MAKAIAS and Dr. G. Maheshwari, Director-in-Charge, MAKAIAS among others attended the inaugural session.
