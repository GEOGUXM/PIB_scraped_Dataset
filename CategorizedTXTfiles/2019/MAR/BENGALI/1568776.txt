      MoU signed with Japan to refurbish the Japan Gallery of Rabindra Bharati Museum at Jorasanko Thakurbari
      Posted On: 13 MAR 2019 6:13PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, March 13, 2019


    The Government of Japan is donating US $ 31,891 (About Rs 22.50 Lakhs) to the Rabindra Bharati University to renovate and modernize the Japan Gallery of Rabindra Bharati Museum at Jorasanko Thakurbari in Kolkata. A MoU was signed for this purpose between Consul General of Japan in Kolkata Mr. Masayuki Taga and Vice-Chancellor of Rabindra Bharati University Prof. Sabyasachi Basu Ray Chaudhury, here today. The fund will be utilised for refurbishing the Japan Gallery of the Museum within a year.


    Speaking on the occasion, the Consul-General of Japan said, this move will rejuvenate and strengthen the historical relations between the two rich cultures of both the countries. He said, Tagore is greatly revered as an important figure in bilateral and cultural history of India and Japan. Stating that Tagore visited Japan four times in the years 1916, 1917, 1924 and 1929, Mr Taga said, the Japan Gallery which was started 12 years ago and now, in the second phase of the project, the renovated and refurbished gallery will further strengthen Indo-Japan ties.
