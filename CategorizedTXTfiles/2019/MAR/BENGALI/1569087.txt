      The Jadavpur Parliamentary Constituency in West Bengal Comprises Maximum Number of Third Gender Electors for 2019 Lok Sabha Poll
      Posted On: 19 MAR 2019 4:08PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, March 19, 2019


    Out of total number of 6,97,60,868 electors in West Bengal for 2019 Lok Sabha poll, the total number of third gender electors is 1426. In the last Lok Sabha election, i.e., in 2014, there was no clear mention of third gender in the phase-wise elector and polling station’s list. They were mentioned as ‘Others’ in that list.


    It is to be mentioned that Supreme Court in a landmark judgement, declared transgender people as ‘third gender’ and affirmed that the fundamental rights granted under the Constitution of India will be equally applicable to transgender people in 2014 and on December 17, 2018, the Lok Sabha passed the Union Government’s Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Bill, 2018. On September 6, 2018, the Supreme Court decriminalised homosexuality by declaring Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) unconstitutional.


    According to the information of the Chief Electoral Officer’s office, in the city, the number of third gender electors in the State in 2019, is maximum in Jadavpur Parliamentary Constituency. The number is 74. Raigunj is next in position so far third gender electors are considered. The number in this Parliamentary constituency is 73. Diamond Harbour Parliamentary constituency is third in position so far as the number of third gender electors are concerned. Here, the number is 61.


    Bishnupur (SC) Parliamentary constituency has the lowest number of third gender electors. Here, the number of such electors is 8.


    It is to be mentioned that this year the total number of male & female electors is 3,57,83,463 and 3,39,75,979 respectively.
