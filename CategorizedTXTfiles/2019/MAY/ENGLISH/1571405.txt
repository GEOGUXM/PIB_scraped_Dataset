      Election Commission

      Kodarma, Ranchi, Khunti and Hazaribagh Parliamentary Constituencies in Jharkhand to go to Polls in Phase V of General Elections 2019
      
      11.29% increase in Electors in these four PCs since last General Elections
      
      1,09,025 electors in 18-19 years age group in Phase V in Jharkhand
      
      14 candidates contesting from Kodarma PC, 20 from Ranchi PC, 11 from Khunti PC and 16 from Hazaribagh PC
      Posted On: 01 MAY 2019 3:59PM by PIB Delhi


    I n the Fifth phase of General Elections 2019, Kodarma, Ranchi, Khunti and Hazaribagh Parliamentary Constituencies (PC) in Jharkhand would be going to polls on 6th May. Kodarma (PC No. 5), Ranchi (PC No. 8) and Hazaribagh (PC No. 14) are General seats, whereas Khunti (PC No. 11) is an ST seat.


    As per the data sourced from CEO (Jharkhand) website, there are a total of 65,87,028 electors in the four PCs, out of which 34,42,266 are male electors, 31,44,679 female electors and 83 third gender electors. There are 1,09,025 electors in the age group of 18-19 years in Phase V in Jharkhand.     The data sourced from ECI website shows that in General Election 2014, there were a total of 59,18,883 electors in these four PCs. This number has grown to 65,87,028 during General Elections 2019, which is an increase of 6,68,145 (11.29%) electors since last General Elections.


    An interesting fact for Phase V of General Elections is that in Jharkhand, the Kodarma PC is the largest PC in terms of population (34,05,027), whereas, the Khunti PC is the smallest PC in terms of population (19,86,808) as well as in terms of size of electorate (11,74,643).


    In Phase V, from different Political Parties, 14 candidates are contesting from Kodarma PC, 20 candidates from Ranchi PC, 11 candidates from Khunti PC and 16 candidates from Hazaribagh PC.
