      73.97% Voter Turnout in Fifth Phase of Lok Sabha Poll in West Bengal
      Posted On: 06 MAY 2019 8:43PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, May 6, 2019


    Seven Lok Sabha constituencies of West Bengal went for poll in the fifth phase of General Election, today. In this phase of election, 51 constituencies across seven States, including seven constituencies of West Bengal-Bangaon (SC), Barrackpur, Howrah, Uluberia, Sreerampur, Hooghly and Arambag (SC) had poll. The voter turnout is 73.97% till 5 pm. During the said time, the poll percentage of Baharampur was 71.97, whereas, the percentage in Bangaon (SC), Barrackpur,Howrah, Uluberia, Sreerampur, Hooghly and Arambag (SC) are 76.18, 71.28, 67.59, 77.57, 73.31, 76.14, and 75.73 respectively. Total 11,691,889 electors exercised their franchise, said the Chief Electoral Officer (CEO), West Bengal, Dr Aariz Aftab in a press meet today in Kolkata. There was also a bye-election in the Uluberia Purba Assembly constituency also, where the average polling percentage was 79.90 till 5 pm.


    There was Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF) in every polling booth in West Bengal in this phase of poll.


    Dr Aftab stated that barring few stray incidents, the polling in this phase was peaceful.


    According to Shri S N Gupta, the Additional Director General (Law and Order), West Bengal police who was also present in the press meet, five FIRs have been lodged and 42 persons arrested as preventive measures for specific cases. He added that the total number of persons injured in stray incidents were 12.
