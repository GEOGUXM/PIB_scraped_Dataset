      9 Lok Sabha Constituencies of West Bengal to go to Poll on May 19 in Seventh Phase of General Election to Lok Sabha, 2019
      Posted On: 14 MAY 2019 6:13PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, May 14, 2019


    Nine Lok Sabha constituencies of West Bengal will have polling in the seventh and last phase of General Election, 2019 on May 19, 2019 (Sunday). In this phase of election, 59 constituencies across seven States and one Union Territory, including Bihar, Himachal Pradesh, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal and Chandigarh will go for poll. The nine constituencies of West Bengal are Dum Dum, Barasat, Basirhat, Jaynagar (SC), Mathurapur (SC), Diamond Harbour, Jadavpur, Kolkata Dakshin and Kolkata Uttar. The polling in West Bengal will start at 7 am and continue till 6 pm. The Chief Electoral Officerâ€™s Office, West Bengal has taken adequate measures to have free and fair election in the State. There will be 710 companies of Central Armed Police Force in this phase of poll for providing 100 per cent coverage including 512 Quick Response Teams (QRTs). This apart, Shri Vivek Dube, the Special Central Police Observer will travel to all the constituencies from May 15 to 17, 2019 to review the poll arrangements.


    A total number of 111 candidates from 11 political parties including Independents and other registered parties are going to try their fortunes in this phase of voting in West Bengal. Out of these 111 candidates, 17 are women. There are 17,042 polling stations in West Bengal in this phase of election. The total number of voters in this phase is 14,963,064 out of which 7,698,023 are males 7,264,664 females and 377 belonging to the third gender. The number of electors are maximum in Jadavpur constituency (18, 16,098) with 9, 09,061 male and 9, 06,962 female voters. The number of third gender electors is maximum in Jadavpur constituency (75).


    It is to be noted that polling has been done in 33 constituencies of West Bengal so far.


    The Commission meanwhile has impressed upon all the observers to remain on constant vigil and ensure that the polls are conducted in an absolutely free, fair and credible manner.
