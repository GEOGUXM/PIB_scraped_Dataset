      8 Lok Sabha Constituencies of West Bengal to go to Poll On May 12 in Sixth Phase of General Election to Lok Sabha, 2019
      Posted On: 08 MAY 2019 2:49PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, May 8, 2019


    Eight Lok Sabha constituencies of West Bengal will have polling in the sixth phase of General Election, 2019 on May 12, 2019 (Sunday). In this phase of election, 59 constituencies across seven States, including Bihar, Haryana, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, NCT of Delhi and West Bengal will go for poll. The eight constituencies of West Bengal include Tamluk, Kanthi, Ghatal, Jhargram (ST), Medinipur, Purulia, Bankura and Bishnupur (SC). The polling in West Bengal will start at 7 am and continue till 6 pm. The Chief Electoral Officerâ€™s Office, West Bengal has taken adequate measures to have free and fair election in the State. There will be 683 companies of Central Armed Police Force in this phase of poll.


    A total number of 82 candidates from 11 political parties including Independents and other registered parties are going to try their fortunes in this phase of voting in West Bengal. Out of these 82 candidates, five are women. There are 15,428 polling stations in West Bengal in this phase of election. The total number of voters in this phase is 13,369,749 out of which 6,833,909 are males 5,535,721 females and 119 belonging to the third gender. The number of electors are maximum in Ghatal constituency (17, 97,685) with 9, 21,050 male and 8,76,615 female voters. The number of third gender electors is maximum in Tamluk (33).


    It is to be noted that polling has been done in 25 constituencies of West Bengal so far. Apart from the above-mentioned eight constituencies of the State which will go to poll in the sixth phase of poll, nine other constituencies will be left for polling which will be covered in the seventh phase on May 19, 2019. The result will be declared on May 23, 2019.


    The Commission meanwhile has impressed upon all the observers to remain on constant vigil and ensure that the polls are conducted in an absolutely free, fair and credible manner.
