      Ministry of Steel

      SAIL earned net profit of Rs. 616 Crore in third quarter of 2018-19
      Posted On: 08 FEB 2019 3:53PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata: February 8, 2019


    Steel Authority of India Limited (SAIL) declared a profit (Profit After Tax) of Rs 616 Crore in the third quarter of 2018-19 (October-December 2018). The profit figure stood at Rs 43 Crore during the corresponding period last year. The Company has managed to better its performance over the previous quarter as well by more than 11% when it stood at Rs. 554 crore. The turnover for Q3 FY’19 improved by 3% over corresponding period last year, which stood at Rs 15,660 Crore vis-à-vis Rs 15,190 Crore during Q3 FY’18. The Earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortization in Q3 FY’19 was Rs 2,653 Crore, an improvement of 70% over the same period last year, which was Rs 1,560 Crore in Q3 FY’18. The improvements in these indicators over the corresponding period last year reflect overall progress on all production parameters and financial health of the Company. The Company’s performance on these indicators during the nine months period (April – December’18) was also higher compared to the figures of corresponding period last year, that points at a steady and sustainable growth in the overall performance.


    The Company is focusing on ramping up production so as to meet a target of rated capacities at all the new units installed under modernization and expansion. Enhancing the volumes will also bring down the cost of production which will improve the performance as a whole. During the third quarter of 2018-19, SAIL recorded the best ever quarterly Crude Steel production of 4.3 Million Tonnes (MT), a 10% growth over corresponding period last year.The third quarter of this financial year also recorded the best ever quarterly Saleable Steel production at 3.8 MT, a 5% growth over the third quarter of 2017-18. On the techno-economic parameters, Blast Furnace Productivity registered a growth of 16% in the third quarter of 2018-19 compared to the previous quarter. Likewise, Coke Rate and Specific Energy Consumption during the quarter also registered a growth of 3% and 2% respectively over previous quarter.


    Speaking on the occasion, SAIL Chairman Shri Anil Kumar Chaudhary said, potential of steel consumption in domestic market is huge. The company is focussed on meeting requirements of all sectors while enhancing volumes, he said. Higher production of value added steel is another focus area and the PSU is committed to provide end-to-end customer services, he further said. Pointing at the current volatility in steel prices, the Chairman said, despite various factors including cheap imports, the prices are expected to improve in coming days considering the high input costs.
