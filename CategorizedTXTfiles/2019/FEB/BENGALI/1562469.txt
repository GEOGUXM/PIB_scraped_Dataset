      Ministry of Mines

      Development of Chapri- Sidheswar Project & Reopening of Rakha Mine
      Posted On: 03 FEB 2019 9:05PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, February 03, 2019.


    The groundbreaking ceremony for construction of a concentrator plant at Rakha, initiating Chapri-Sidheswar mine, development of a new mine and reopening the Rakha mine project was held at Ghatshila Unit of Hindustan Copper Limited (HCL) on Saturday. The dignitaries present on the occasion included Shri Raghubar Das, Chief Minister of Jharkhand, Shri Bidyut Barman Mahato, MP (LS), Jamshedpur, Shri Santosh Sharma, CMD of HCL among others


    Shri Sharma, CMD, Hindustan Copper Limited, stated that Chapri and Rakha projects have combined production capacity of 50 lakh tonnes with an investment of Rs 1000 crore and will be implemented with “Zero Mine Waste” Philosophy. He further informed that the above projects will create direct job opportunities for around 8000 people of the area, considering the opportunity to further increase the resource base of Copper mineral in Jharkhand. HCL has finalized a plan to undertake depth exploration upto 1000 meters from surface in its exiting lease area in next two years at a cost of Rs 100.0 crore, Shri Sharma said.


    Hindustan Copper Limited, the only Copper mining Company in India, in its unit located at Ghatsila has planned to implement total five mine expansion projects to increase the mine capacity from current 4 to 72 lakh tonnes with total capital investment of Rs 1600 crore. The project is expected to take five years for completion. Successful implementation of the project will also reduce country's dependence on imported copper concentrate.
