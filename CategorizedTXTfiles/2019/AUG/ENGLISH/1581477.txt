      Ministry of Defence

      Army Enhances Flood Relief & Rescue Operations in Maharashtra & Karnataka
      Posted On: 07 AUG 2019 7:26PM by PIB Delhi


    The flood situation in Maharashtra and Karnataka continues to be of concern due to incessant and heavy spells of rain and release of water from major reservoirs over the last few days. The Indian Army is assisting the Maharashtra and Karnataka state authorities in carrying out relief and rescue operations in flood hit areas.


    As on 07 August 2019, a total of 16 Columns and 12 Engineer Task Forces comprising of almost 1000 Army personnel have been employed in Belgaum, Bagalkot and Raichur dist of Karnataka and Raigad, Kohlapur and Sangli district of Maharashtra to carryout flood relief operations.


    A total of around 500 persons have been rescued, provided medical aid and distributed with food packets. The rescue operations by Army personnel are presently underway in the Villages of Birwadi and Asankoli in Raigad District, Mouje Digraj, Haripur and Nandre Village of Sangli and Kohlapur District.
