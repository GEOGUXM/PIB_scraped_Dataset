      Campaign for Second Phase of General Election to Lok Sabha, 2019 Ends Today
      
      3 Lok Sabha Constituencies of West Bengal among 97 across 12 States and 1 UT to go to Polls in Second Phase on April 18
      Posted On: 16 APR 2019 4:30PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, April 16, 2019


    The campaigning for the second phase of General Election to the Lok Sabha, 2019 ended today. In this phase of election, 97 Lok Sabha constituencies across 12 States and one Union Territory, including three constituencies of West Bengal-Jalpaiguri (SC), Darjeeling and Raigunj will have poll. The date of poll is April 18, 2019 (Thursday). The polling in West Bengal will start at 7 am and continue till 6 pm.


    The total number of candidates contesting in this phase is 1629, out of which, 42 are from West Bengal from 11 parties. Out of the 42 candidates, three are women. There are 5390 polling booths in West Bengal in this phase of election. The number of electors in this phase is nearly 157934518, out of which the electoral number is nearly 4932346 in West Bengal. The number of male voters in this State is nearly 2522887, the number of females, 2409372 and third gender nearly 87.


    It is to be noted that Chief Election Commissioner Shri Sunil Arora and the Election Commissioners Shri Ashok Lavasa and Shri Sushil Chandra reviewed the poll arrangements in specific constituencies of West Bengal going to polls in second and third phases on Monday (April 15, 2019) through video-conferencing with General Observers, Police Observers and Expenditure Observers deployed in three Parliamentary constituencies in West Bengal, in second phase, namely Jalpaiguri (SC), Darjeeling and Raigunj as well as Balurghat, Maldaha Uttar, Maldaha Dakshin, Jangipur, Murshidabad in third phase of poll.


    The Commission impressed upon all the observers to remain on constant vigil and ensure that the polls are conducted in absolutely free, fair and credible manner.
