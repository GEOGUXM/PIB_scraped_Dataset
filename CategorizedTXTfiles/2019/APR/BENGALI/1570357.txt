      2 Parliamentary Constituencies of West Bengal amongst 91 to Undergo Poll tomorrow in First Phase of General Election, 2019
      Posted On: 10 APR 2019 4:23PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, April 10, 2019


    In the first phase of General Election, 2019, polling will be held tomorrow in 91 out of 543 Lok Sabha constituencies spread over 20 States and Union Territories including West Bengal.


    Polling will be held in two constituencies of the State in the first phase- Coochbehar (SC) and Alipurduars (ST). The polling in these two constituencies will start from 7 am and continue till 6 pm.


    Eighteen candidates are in the fray from these two constituencies of the State, out of which 16 are male and two female. There are 3844 polling stations in these two constituencies, out of which 2010 are in Coochbehar (SC) and 1834 in Alipurduars (ST). The Chief Electoral Officer, Dr Aariz Aftab informed that there will be adequate number of armed police personnel in all the polling booths.


    Total 3454276 general electors will exercise their franchises for the two constituencies of the State, out of which 1773196 will be male, 1681051 will be female and 29 third gender. 8307 service voters will also cast their votes in these two constituencies, out of which 8079 are male and 228 are female.


    The Additional Chief Electoral Officer, Shri Sanjoy Basu said, the Election Commission of India (ECI) has already issued instructions regarding alternate documents, other than Electoral Photo Identity Cards (EPIC) to be produced at polling stations for casting vote. They are- passport, driving license, Service Identity Cards with photograph issued to employees by Central/State Govt/ PSUs/Public Limited Companies, passbooks with photographs issued by Bank/Post Office, PAN Card, Smart Card issued by RGI under NPR, MNREGA Job Card, Health Insurance Smart Card issued under the scheme of Ministry of Labour, pension document with photograph, official identity cards issued to MPs/MLAs/MLCs and AADHAR Card.
