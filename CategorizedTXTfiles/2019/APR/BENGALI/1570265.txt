      CEO Flags off Howrah Express
      Posted On: 09 APR 2019 3:33PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, April 9, 2019


    The Chief Electoral Officer (CEO) of West Bengal, Dr Aariz Aftab flagged off 12833/12834 Howrah Express of South Eastern Railway Zone on Monday (April 8, 2019) night from Howrah station. The train running between Ahmedabad and Howrah covers a distance of 2,087 kms. The train carries voter awareness messages in the outer parts of its body with, the photo of both national and State icon. It is to be mentioned that Saina Nehwal is the national icon for the General election, 2019, whereas Jeeja Ghosh, the disability activist is the icon for West Bengal. The messages are written both in Hindi and English.


    The train is a superfast express train and takes 37 hours 35 minutes to reach Ahmedabad covering six States with 63 stops. The States covered include Gujarat, Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Jharkhand and West Bengal.


    Some of the major stations are Surat, Jalgaon Junction, Akola, Nagpur, Durg, Bilaspur Junction, Champa, Raigarh, Jharsuguda Junction, Rourkela, Chakradharpur, Tatanagar, Kharagpur and Santragachhi. There are 17 stops in the way with the stoppage of five minutes or more. As the train travels across these six States, people will be encouraged to click a photograph with the train and upload it on social media for further spreading the message of voter awareness.


    Meanwhile, voter awareness audio-visual messages are now regularly displayed in the platforms of Metro Rail as well as some of the malls of the city.
