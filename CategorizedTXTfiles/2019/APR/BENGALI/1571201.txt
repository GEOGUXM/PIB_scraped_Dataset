      8 Women among 68 Contestants from West Bengal in Fourth Phase of General Election to Lok Sabha, 2019
      Posted On: 26 APR 2019 3:56PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, April 26, 2019


    Seventy one Parliamentary constituencies of the country across nine States including eight from West Bengal will go for poll in the fourth phase of General Election to Lok Sabha, 2019. This phase of poll is scheduled on April 29, 2019 (Monday). Total 68 candidates from West Bengal representing 11 parties including eight females will be in the fray in this phase of poll. The constituencies include Baharampur, Krishnanagar, Ranaghat (SC), Bardhaman Purba (SC), Bardhaman-Durgapur, Asansol, Bolepur (SC) and Birbhum. The polling will start at 7 am and continue till 6 pm.


    The total number of electors from West Bengal in this phase is 13,456,991. Out of this, 6,906,199 are male, 6,550,004 female and 288 belonging to third gender. There are 15,277 polling stations in this phase of poll in West Bengal.


    Meanwhile, the Election Commission of India (ECI) has taken adequate measures to have free and fair poll in this phase and as stated by Chief Electoral Officer (CEO), Dr Aariz Aftab it is evident that there will be Central Armed police Forces (CAPF) in 98 per cent of the booths. The Special Observer appointed by ECI, Shri Ajay V Nayak too opined that in the remaining four phases of the ongoing Lok Sabha poll, measures will be taken to provide CAPF in cent per cent of the polling booths. Besides, strict vigil will be there also so as to keep the sanctity of the entire poll process.
