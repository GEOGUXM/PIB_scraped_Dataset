      Howrah Express of South Eastern Railway Zone, Indian Railways to Carry Voter Awareness Messages
      Posted On: 01 APR 2019 3:02PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, April 1, 2019


    Election Commission of India and Indian Railways have join hands to utilise four long-distance trains to carry voter awareness messages. Howrah Express of South Eastern Railway Zone running between Ahmedabad and Howrah is one of the trains among these four.


    Howrah Express is a daily superfast express train which covers 2087 kms in roughly 37 hours 35 minutes covering six States and having 63 stops. The States covered include Gujarat, Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Jharkhand and West Bengal.


    Some of the major stations are Surat, Jalgaon Junction, Akola, Nagpur, Durg, Bilaspur Junction, Champa, Raigarh, Jharsuguda Junction, Rourkela, Chakradharpur, Tatanagar, Kharagpur and Santragachhi. There are 17 stops in the way with the stoppage of five minutes or more. As the train travels across these six States, people will be encouraged to click a photograph with the train and upload it on social media for further spreading the message of voter awareness.
