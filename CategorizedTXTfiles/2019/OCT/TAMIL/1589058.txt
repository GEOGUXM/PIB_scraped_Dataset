      Income tax Department withdraws 1072 appeal cases
      Posted On: 24 OCT 2019 4:18PM by PIB Chennai


    The Income Tax Department, Tamil Nadu has withdrawn a total of 1072 number cases as on date in which 716 is on High Court and 356 is on Income Tax Appellate Tribunal. The number of Department appeals pending before the ITAT benches and Hon’ble High Court as on date are approximately 1565 and 4187 respectively.


    In a series of tax payer friendly measures, the Central Board of Direct Taxes, recently announced enhancement of the monetary limits for filing appeals by the Income Tax Department. Monetary limits for filing of appeal in Income Tax Appellate Tribunal was increased to Rs. 50,00,000 from Rs. 20,00,000, likewise monetary limits for High court was raised to Rs. 1,00,00,000 from Rs. 50,00,000 and monetary limits for Supreme Court was raised to Rs. 2,00,00,000 from Rs. 1,00,00,000. This has come to effect from 8.8.2019.


    The above enhancement of monetary limits for filing appeals by the Department and the withdrawal of the appeals accordingly has enabled redressal of the tax payers grievances and effective reduction in litigations.


    The Department has also identified 52 cases for withdrawal from the Supreme Court as a part of the above exercise. The Income Tax Department, Tamil Nadu region has implemented the above mentioned tax payer friendly initiative in word and spirit and is also the forerunner in the country in implementation of the said exercise.


    The Commissioner of Income Tax(Judicial), Chennai on behalf of the Principal Chief Commissioner of Income Tax (TN & Puducherry) has coordinated with the ITAT and Hon’ble High Court for withdrawal of appeals expeditiously to reduce the litigation, says Shri. Durgesh Sumrott, Commissioner of Income Tax (Judicial), Chennai in a press release issued today.


      The Income Tax Appellate Tribunal, Tamilnadu has four benches and all are situated in Chennai which cater to the entire Tamil Nadu and Puducherry region.
