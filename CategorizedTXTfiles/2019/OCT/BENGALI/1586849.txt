      President visits the National Institute of Locomotor Disabilities at Kolkata
      Posted On: 01 OCT 2019 4:14PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, October 1, 2019


    The President of India Shri Ram Nath Kovind visited the National Institute of Locomotor Disabilities (NILD) here today and interacted with the divyangjans admitted in the institute, particularly differently abled children.


    The President was received by the Governor of West Bengal, Shri Jagdeep Dhankar, the Union Minister of Social Justice and Empowerment, Dr.Thawar Chand Gehlot, the Minister of Technical Education, Training and Skill Development, Government of West Bengal, Shri Purnendu Basu along with senior officials. The President was accompanied by First Lady Smt Savita Kovind. The President appreciated the institute’s endeavours in providing e-rickshaw for facilitating commuting of patients from the nearest bus stop to NILD campus.


    Shri Kovind visited the ‘Early Intervention Multi Sensory Unit’ where children with autism, Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) and learning disabilities are treated.


    The President interacted with children, their parents and caregivers enquiring how the children are given special therapies to revive their cognitive abilities. After visiting the‘Female and Children Ward’, the President proceeded to the exhibition, where various paintings and handicrafts by divyangjans were showcased. Lauding their talents, he emphasised on the publicity of the art-works and asserted that efforts should be made to include the divyangjans in the mainstream of society.


    Shri Kovind planted a Neem sapling at the premises of the institute.
