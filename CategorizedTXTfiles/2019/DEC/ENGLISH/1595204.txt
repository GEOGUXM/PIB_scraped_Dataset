      Ministry of Health and Family Welfare

      Upgradation of Medical Colleges/Hospitals
      Posted On: 06 DEC 2019 1:36PM by PIB Delhi


    The Ministry of Health & Family Welfare administers a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for ‘Establishment of new Medical Colleges attached with existing district/referral hospitals’. The Phase-III of the Scheme has been approved by the Cabinet on 28.8.2019 to establish 75 more new medical colleges. The State/UT Governments have been requested to send their proposals under this Scheme along with Detailed Project Report as per Scheme guidelines for consideration by the Ministry.

    Under National Health Mission, technical and financial support is provided to States/ Union Territories for strengthening their healthcare system including strengthening of district hospitals. More than 22,000 delivery points have been identified and additional funds for strengthening these facilities have been provided.


    Further, Pradhan Mantry Swasthya Suraksha Yojna (PMSSY) is a Central Sector Scheme that has two components:

     1. Setting up of new AIIMs; and
     2. Up-gradation of existing Government Medical Colleges/ Institutions (GMCIs)

    Upgradation of existing GMCIs under PMSSY involves construction of Super Speciality Block (SSB)/ Trauma Centre and procurement of Medical Equipments. Under PMSSY, construction of Trauma Centre/ Emergency Ward has been approved at the following existing GMCIs:

     1. Government Medical College, Salem, Tamil Nadu
     2. IMS, BHU, Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
     3. Jawaharlal Nehru Medical College of Aligarh Muslim University, Aligarh, Uttar Pradesh
     4. Nizam Institute of Medical Sciences, Hyderabad, Telangana
     5. Government Medical College, Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir
     6. Vijayanagar Institute of Medical Sciences, Bellary
     7. Kozhikode Medical College, Kerala
     8. Govt. Medical College, Malda, West Bengal

    PMSSY is being implemented by the Ministry directly through Executive Agencies and Procurement Support Agency. Funds are released directly to these agencies.


    The Minister of State (Health and Family Welfare), Sh Ashwini Kumar Choubey stated this in a written reply in the Lok Sabha here today.
