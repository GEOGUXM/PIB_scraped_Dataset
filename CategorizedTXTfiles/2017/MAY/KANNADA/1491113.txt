    NACEN TO IMPART GST TRAINING


    Bengaluru, May 29, 2017


    Inaugurating the National Academy of Customs Excise and Narcotics (NACEN) Campus in the city, today, Shri Arun Jaitley, Union Finance Minister said that indirect taxation regime in the country will play a key role and is undergoing a vital change. The multiple taxation system which is governed now is transformed into Goods and Service Tax (GST) and all the taxes are amalgamated and the new GST will come into effect from July 1, 2017, he said. GST is an efficient tax system which not only checks tax evasion but it also help evolving India to become very strong society. New indirect tax is also a product of federal India, he added.


     1. Finance Minister said that the centre and state jointly administer and decide the taxes. Coordination between taxation authority of central and state is also important and tax training academy of this kind, which now the NACEN come up in the prominent city like Bengaluru to impart training to officers of central and state governments and PSUs has to play a vital role, he felt.


     1. on the occasion, Smt.Vanaja N.Sarna, Chairperson, CBEC highlighted the contributions of NACEN. Shri D.P.Nagendra Kumar, Principal Director General, National Academy of Customs Indirect Taxes and Narcotics gave a overview of the new NACEN Complex. Earlier, Shri S.Ramesh, Member (Admn.), CBIC welcomed the dignitaries and Shri P.K.Dash, Pr.Additonal Director General, NACIN proposed vote of thanks.
