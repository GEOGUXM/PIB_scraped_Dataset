      Ministry of Tourism

      Rs.727.16 crore sanctioned for 24 projects under PRASHAD scheme: Shri. K J Alphons
      Posted On: 06 AUG 2018 5:38PM by PIB Delhi


    The Ministry of Tourism under its schemes PRASHAD- National Mission on Pilgrimage Rejuvenation and Spiritual, Heritage Augmentation Drive provides central financial assistance to State Governments/UT Administrations/Central Agencies for development of tourism infrastructure in the country


    Since itâ€™s inception in the year 2014-15, 28 sites/destinations in 21 states have been selected for infrastructure development under PRASHAD and total 24 projects have been sanctioned in 16 states with the approved cost of Rs.727.16 crore and an amount of Rs.270.24 crore has been released for these projects till date.


    The detail of the projects undertaken by Ministry of Tourism for implementation in the state of Andhra Pradesh under PRASHAD Scheme is given below:

     1. Development of Amaravati Town, Guntur District as a Tourist Destination sanctioned in the year 2015-16 with the cost of Rs. 28.36 crore and an amount of Rs. 22.69 crore has been released till date.
     2. Development of Srisailam Tample, Kurnool District in the year 2017-18 with the cost of Rs. 47.45 crore and an amount of Rs. 22.75 crore has been released till date.

    This information was given by the Minister of State (I/C) for Tourism Shri. K. J. Alphons, in a written reply in Lok Sabha today.
