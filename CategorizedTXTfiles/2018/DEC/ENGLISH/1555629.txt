      Ministry of New and Renewable Energy

      Solar Energy Production in the year 2018-19 was 21.365 Billion Units
      Posted On: 12 DEC 2018 3:59PM by PIB Delhi


    Central Electricity Authority (CEA) has reported that the total solar energy production in the year 2018-19 was 21.365 Billion Units (BUs), as on 31.10.2018.


    The year-wise details of solar energy production since, 2015 are given as under: -

                                                    
S. No  Year     Generation from Solar Projects (BUs)
                                                    
                                                    
                                                    
1.     2015-16  7.448                               
                                                    
                                                    
                                                    
2.     2016-17  13.499                              
                                                    
                                                    
                                                    
3.     2017-18  25.871                              
                                                    
                                                    
                                                    
4.     2018-19  21.365 (as on 31.10.2018)           
                                                    
                                                    

    Government have taken various steps to increase generation of renewable energy. These inter-alia, include the following:

     1. Announcement of a target of installing 175 GW of renewable energy capacity by March, 2022;
     2. Declaration of trajectory for Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO) up to the year 2021-22;
     3. Fiscal and financial incentives such as Capital Subsidy, Viability gap funding (VGF), accelerated depreciation benefits etc;
     4. Permitting 100% Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) under the automatic route in renewable energy sector;
     5. Guidelines for procurement of solar and wind power through tariff based competitive bidding process;
     6. Waiving of Inter State Transmission System Charges and losses for inter-state sale of solar and wind power for projects to be commissioned up to March, 2022;
     7. Raising funds from bilateral and multilateral finance and development institutions;
     8. Implementation of Green Energy Corridor project to facilitate integration of large scale renewable generation capacity addition.

    This was informed by Minister of State (I/C) for New and Renewable Energy and PowerShri R. K Singh in a written reply in the Rajya Sabha today.
