      Ministry of Women and Child Development

      Government is implementing various schemes/programmes for welfare of women and children across the country
      Posted On: 08 FEB 2018 6:34PM by PIB Delhi


    The Ministry of Women and Child Development is implementing various schemes/programmes for welfare of women and children across the country. The schemes are:

     1. Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP)
     2. Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) Scheme
     3. Scheme for Adolescent Girls (SAG)
     4. Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)
     5. Working Women Hostels (WWH)
     6. Ujjawala
     7. Gender Budgeting
     8. Child Protection Scheme (CPS)
     9. Swadhar Greh
    10. National Creche Scheme (NCS)
    11. Rashtriya Mahila Kosh (RMK)
    12. One Stop Centre and
    13. Women Helpline.

    T he above mentioned schemes/programmes are being implemented through State Governments and some of the schemes such as Ujjawala, Swadhar Greh, WWH, National Creche Scheme and RMK are implemented through NGOs. The State/UT-wise details of beneficiaries covered under these schemes/programmes are given at Annexure-I to Annexure-VII.


    The Ministry holds regular review meetings with Women and Child Development Departments of State Governments/UT Administrations to discuss the issues for effective implementation of the programmes and schemes to expedite beneficiary coverage and proper fund utilization. Regional level consultations are also held to discuss issues concerning implementation of schemes. For some schemes, t he annual inspections are being carried out jointly by the Centre and State Govt. officials. The monitoring reports received from States/UTs are analyzed in the Ministry. The problem areas are communicated to concerned States/UTs for taking corrective actions. In addition, regular State visits are conducted by the Ministry’s officials in order to understand the implementation problems being faced by States/UTs. . For effective implementation of schemes of MWCD run by NGOs and to check funds utilization, the schemes have in-built monitoring mechanism. Also, the regular follow-up is done by the Ministry and the States/UTs for every sanction of fund to the NGOs. The schemes are also periodically reviewed for their proper and better implementation.
