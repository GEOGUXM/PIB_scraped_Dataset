      Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports

      Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan to organize Kashmiri Youth Exchange Programme in Kolkata
      Posted On: 17 SEP 2018 3:37PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, September 17, 2018.


    Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan(NYKS), West Bengal, Kolkata will organize Kashmiri Youth Exchange Programme (KEYP), with the assistance of Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India at Ramchandra Mission Ashrama, near Heritage School, Chowbhaga, Anandapur, Kolkata from September 18 to 22, 2018,said Shri Nabin Kumar Naik, State Director, NKYP here today. He added that the inaugural function of the said programme will be held tomorrow, September 18, 2018 at 11 AM in the venue mentioned above. He said that Minister of Consumer Affairs, Government of West Bengal, Shri Sadhan Pandey and Additional Director General (M&C), of Press Information Bureau, Government of India, Kolkata, Miss Jane Namchu will also be present on the occasion.


    A total of 132 youth under the age group of 18 to 22 years from districts of Anantnag, Kupwara, Baramulla, Budgam, Srinagar and Pulwama will participate in the programme. The objective of this programme is to orient, sensitize and provide opportunities to the participants to act as proponents of national unity, integrity and peace among the young people of Kashmir valley. They will visit different places of cultural, industrial, historical, religious and educational importance.


    North-East Youth Exchange programme and Tribal Exchange programme will also be held by NYKS in coming days. Under such programmes, youth will visit different places of the country to understand the socio-economic development, cultural ethos, language, lifestyles of the people depicting unity in diversity aspect of our national life and social and financial inclusion programmes of government.
