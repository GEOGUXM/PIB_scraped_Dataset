      Monthly Review of the Account of the Government of India for the month of February 2018 for the Financial Year 2017-18; Government of India has received Rs.12,83,472 crore (79.09% of corresponding RE 17-18 of Total Receipts) up ​ ​to February 2018 comprising Rs. 10,35,546 crore Tax Revenue (Net to Centre), Rs.1,42,132 crore of Non Tax Revenue and Rs.1,05,794 crore of Non Debt Capital Receipts.
      Posted On: 28 MAR 2018 2:39PM by PIB Delhi


    The Monthly Account of the Union Government of India up to February 2018 has been consolidated and reports published. The highlights are given below:-


    The Government of India has received Rs.12,83,472 crore (79.09% of corresponding RE 17-18 of Total Receipts) upto February 2018 comprising


    Rs. 10,35,546 crore Tax Revenue (Net to Centre), Rs.1,42,132 crore of Non Tax Revenue and Rs.1,05,794 crore of Non Debt Capital Receipts. Non Debt Capital Receipts consists of Recovery of Loans (Rs.13,301crore) and Disinvestment of PSUs (Rs. 92,493 crore).


    Rs.5,29,624 crore has been transferred to State Governments as Devolution of Share of Taxes by Government of India in this period which is Rs. 66,039 crore higher than the corresponding period of last year 2016-17.


    Total Expenditure incurred by Government of India is Rs.19,99,171 crore (90.14% of corresponding RE 17-18), out of which Rs.17,02,085 crore is on Revenue Account and Rs.2,97,086 crore is on Capital Account. Out of the total Revenue Expenditure Rs.4,50,729 crore is on account of Interest Payments and Rs.2,27,678 crore is on account of Major Subsidies.
