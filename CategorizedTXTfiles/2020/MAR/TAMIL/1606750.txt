      Gold worth Rs. 27.30 lakhs seized by Customs at Chennai Airport, One Arrested
      Posted On: 17 MAR 2020 4:12PM by PIB Chennai


    On Monday one Kader Maideen,46, of Chennai who had arrived by Emirates flight EK542 was intercepted at exit. The passenger appeared to be walking with difficulty and was not able to take quick strides as if some heavy weight was pulling him down. He was taken up for personal search. One small chain weighing 19 grams valued Rs. 80,579/- was recovered from his pant pocket. On close examination of his slipper sit was noticed that small round cavities were ingeniously carved out the rubber sole of the slippers in which he had concealed gold bits and as such he was having difficulty to walk with heavy slippers. In total 20 gold cut bits weighing 621 grams valued Rs.26.33 lakhs were recovered and seized and Customs Act 1962. Total 640 grams of gold valued at Rs.27.33 lakhs was recovered and seized under Customs Act 1962. Passenger was arrested.


    Further investigation is under progress, according to a press release issued by Commissioner of Customs, Chennai International Airport
