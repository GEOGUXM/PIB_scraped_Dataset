      Ecstasy Pills from Netherlands seized by Chennai Air Customs in foreign postal parcels. Two detained
      Posted On: 09 JUL 2020 12:15PM by PIB Chennai


    Chennai Air Customs has detained two postal parcels suspected to contain narcotics substance, which arrived from Netherlands at Foreign Post Office, Chennai.


    On examination, first parcel was found to contain 490 green colour pills suspected to be MDMA, a narcotics substance. These pills are commonly known as ‘Frosch’ have a smiley one side and contain around 160 mg of MDMA. Second parcel was found to contain 50 orange pills embossed with bull logo known as “Lamborghini” and contain around 200 mg of MDMA. Total 540 MDMA pills valued at Rs 16 lakhs were recovered and seized under the NDPS Act, 1985.


    The parcels were addressed to two different persons in Chennai city. As a follow up action, search were carried out at the addresses and two persons have been detained to ascertain their role in smuggling.


    Ecstasy pills are popular among youth and known as party drugs. During lockdown period, there is a spurt in the seizures related to MDMA pills as lockdown has caused people to stay at home and longer stay at home is creating anxiety.


    Further investigation is in progress, according to a press release issued by Shri.RAJAN CHAUDHARY, Commissioner of Customs, Chennai International Airport.
