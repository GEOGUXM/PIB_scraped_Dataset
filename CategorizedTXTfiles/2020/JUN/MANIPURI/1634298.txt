      Fact Check: No shortage of fertiliser in Manipur
      Posted On: 25 JUN 2020 6:52PM by PIB Imphal


    A report published in a section of press in the state yesterday said that farmers of Seijang, Imphal East were agitated over shortage of fertilisers in the state.


    ( Fix a date so that we can take our own lives: Seijang farmers, Imphal Free Press dated 23.6.2020, link: https://ifp.co.in/fix-a-date-so-that-we-can-take-our-own-lives-seijang-farmers#:~:text=By%20IFP%20Bureau%20%7C%20Jun%2023,%2C%202020%2C%2010%3A20%20p.m.&text=Unable%20to%20get%20their%20hands,they%20can%20take%20their%20lives. )


    Director of Agriculture, Manipur government has since assured the fa r mers that there will not be any shortage of Urea in the state.Speaking to media persons, Director of Agriculture said that the situation is being monitored co n stantly. He added that Manipur requires around 15,000 tonnes of urea every year but the Central Government usually supplies 20,000 tonnes to the State, which is more than sufficient. Chief Minister and Agriculture Minister of Man i pur have already directed the officials to inspect the private Urea dealers and their godowns to ensure smooth supply of fertilisers during this farming season.
