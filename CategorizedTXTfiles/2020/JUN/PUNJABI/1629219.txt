      Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Package (PMGKP) provides financial assistance of Rs 53,248 crore to around 42 crore poor
      Posted On: 03 JUN 2020 7:45PM by PIB Chandigarh


    Chandigarh, June 3, 2020


    Around 42 crore poor people received financial assistance of Rs 53,248 crore under the Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Package (PMGKP). Announced in March by the Finance Minister Smt. Nirmala Sitharaman, Rs 1.7 lakh crore relief package to help poor cope up with the effects during and after lockdown, provides for free food grains and cash payment to women and poor senior citizens and farmers.


    As a result, Rs 16,394 crore front loaded towards payment of the first instalment of Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) to 8.19 crore beneficiaries. Davinder Singh, a farmer from Nangal village in Haryana said he had received Rs 2000 in his account under PM-KISAN. Expressing satisfaction with the scheme, Baljit, another farmer from the same village said that he would use the money received for the purchase of seeds and fertilizer.

    Money has been credited into the women Jan Dhan account holders under PMGKP. In first instalment, Rs 10,029 crore credited to 20.05crore (98.33%) women Jan Dhan account holders. Second instalment has credited Rs 10,315 crore to 20.62 crore (100%) women Jan Dhan account holders. Total Rs 2,814.5 crore disbursed to about 2.81 crore old age persons, widows and disabled persons in two instalments.


    So far 101 Lakh MT of food grains have been lifted by 36 States/UTs for April. 36.93 LMT of foodgrains have been distributed, covering 73.86 crore beneficiaries by 36 States/UTs for April 2020. 32.92 LMT of food grains have been distributed, covering 65.85 crore beneficiaries by 35 States/UTs for May 2020. 3.58 LMT of foodgrains have been distributed, covering 7.16 crore beneficiaries by 17 States/UTs for June 2020. 5.06LMT of Pulses have also been dispatched to various states/UTs. Total 1.91 LMT Pulses have been distributed so far to 17.9 crore household beneficiaries out of 19.4 crore such beneficiaries.
