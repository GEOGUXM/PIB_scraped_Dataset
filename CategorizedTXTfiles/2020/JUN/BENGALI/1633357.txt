      Ministry of Railways

      SER RUNS 1968 TRIPS OF PARCEL EXPRESS TRAINS FOR UNINTERRUPTED SUPPLY OF ESSENTIAL COMMODITIES
      Posted On: 22 JUN 2020 5:27PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, 22 June, 2020


    Indian Railways, aptly called the 'LIFELINE OF THE NATION' is equipped with all facilities to distribute essential commodities to every nook and corner of the country in the wake of steady spread of Coronavirus through movement of Time Tabled Parcel Express Trains. As a part of commitment to the nation during the lockdown, South Eastern Railway (SER) geared up its entire network to run such Time Tabled Parcel Express Trains in different destinations of the country. SER, during this national crisis, had run 1968 trips of such trains carrying essential commodities like, food items, groceries, medicines, medical equipments including PPE masks, sanitisers coveralls, gloves, fish, fruits, cotton goods, gunny bags, vegetables, onion, ginger, garlic and other daily need items to different parts of the country from April 2 to June 20, 2020. SER during the mentioned period, carried 30,544 tonnes of parcel traffic containing 11,50,199 packages to different parts of the country.


    This robust task has come into reality with the proper planning and overwhelming responses from the dedicated workforce of frontline warriors of SER like, loco pilots, guards, gang-men, trackmen, cabin-men, control staff working at stations and divisional headquarters who are putting their exemplary services to the country during this crisis period SER started transporting parcel goods in nine pairs of Special Trains in the path of existing Mail/ Express Trains which were running effect from June 1, 2020 between Howrah-Secunderabad, Howrah-Mumbai, Howrah-Ahmedabad, Howrah-Yesvantpur, Shalimar-Patna, Howrah-Barbil, Howrah- Bhubaneswar, Tatanagar-Danapur and Ranchi-Patna.
