      HDC Achieves an All-time Record in Cargo Handling, Registers 3.25% Growth against National Average

      Posted On: 19 JUN 2020 6:03PM by PIB Kolkata

    Kolkata, 19 June, 2020


    Haldia Dock Complex (HDC) has achieved an all-time record of cargo-handling of 46.67 MMT during 2019-20, thereby registering a 3.25 per cent growth against a national average of 0.82 per cent of all major Port Trusts. The highest-ever cargo (4.63 MT) handled in a single month was achieved in January, 2020. The highest-ever cargo-handling in a single day (203246 MT) was achieved on January 26, 2020. Besides, a single day record handling of 20,320 MT of non-cooking coal in terms of cargo lighterage at sand head on February 3, 2020 has been attained.


    On June 10, 2020 HDC discharged the highest ever single vessel LPG parcel load of 27980 MT. Further, HDC is successful not only in retaining its cargo but also in mobilizing new cargo like Steel at Haldia and had successfully handled 7.27 MMT cargo since April 2020 in the midst of COVID-19 pandemic situation.


    HDC also achieved an all-time high of 24.1 MMT of rail-borne traffic handled during 2019-20, thereby registering a 2.18 per cent growth. The rail-borne traffic handled in the month of May, 2020 increased by 20.8 per cent over that of April, 2020. In order to enhance the efficiency, one new siding has been added and two more are at the advanced stage of construction. Expression of Interest or EOI for development of LPG siding has been floated in May, 2020.


    HDC has also evolved various marketing strategies in an attempt to woo the prospective organisations for investing in Haldia through land-related activities. The Ministry of Shipping, Govt. of India is also in the process of formulating a Township Policy for Kolkata Port Trust.


    The marine operations of HDC were carried out smoothly all along despite COVID-19 pandemic situation and Amphan supercyclone aftermath. A new lay-up berth was commissioned in January, 2020 and a landmark project of LPG-handling through barges via inland waterways is being contemplated. Besides, the river draft has increased whereas the dredging expenditure has decreased in the last few months. This apart, HDC is pursuing actively in night berthing at HOJ-II and is contemplating its implementation shortly.
