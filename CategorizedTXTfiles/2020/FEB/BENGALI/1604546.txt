      Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas

      BS-VI to be implemented from April 1, 2020 across the country.
      Posted On: 27 FEB 2020 5:06PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata: February 27, 2020


    BS-VI fuel standards and BS-VI standards will be implemented in the entire country including West Bengal from April 1, 2020. It will have sulphur content of 10 parts per million (PPM), a reduction from 50 ppm content from the BS-IV specification, informed Shri Pritish Bharat, Executive Director, Indian Oil Corporation Ltd, West Bengal. here today. Interacting with the media persons, Shri Bharat stated that IndianOil refineries, pipelines, storage terminals and retail outlets across the state have been upgraded and quality measures have been put in place to ensure supply of BS-VI fuel.


    In order to ensure quantity and quality of fuel delivery, IndianOil has embarked on a major drive of retail automation across the country. In West Bengal, it has already automated 100% of its RO outlets. Customers sharing their mobile number and vehicle number will be getting e-receipt after fuelling which shall be forwarded to their mobile automatically from automated dispensing unit immediately after fuelling, Shri Bharat said.


    Shri Bharat mentioned that LPG penetration under the Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) has increased from 41.4% as on 01.04.2014 to 97.5% as on 01.02.2020 with an increase of customers by 126 lakhs. In a view to empower women, lady mechanics ar3e being trained and employed in all LPG distributors. 350 lady mechanics have been employed by Indane distributors so far and around 200 more to be trained and employed, Shri Bharat added.
