      Ministry of Communications

      Tales in Baked Clay Tiles Now in Postage Stamps
      Posted On: 17 AUG 2020 5:31PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, August 17, 2020


    The Department of Posts, West Bengal Circle has released a set of seven commemorative postage stamps on ‘Terracotta Temples of India’ recently comprising Madan Mohan Temple, Shyam Rai Temple and Jor Bangla Temple of Bishnupur (Bankura district), Lalji Temple of Kalna (Purba Bardhaman district), Indralath Temple, Ranipur Jharial (Balangir district), Orissa, Nebiya Khera (Kanpur Dehat district), Uttar Pradesh and Lakshman Temple, Sirpur (Mahasamund district), Chattisgarh.


    These postage stamps reflect the rich cultural heritage and the unique architectural tradition of India and West Bengal in particular. The stamps are of Rs 5 and Rs 12 denominations and are available for sale at the philately bureaus across the country, including the Kolkata GPO, Siliguri Head Post Office, Durgapur Head Post office, Darjeeling Head post Office as well as Port Blair head Post office in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
