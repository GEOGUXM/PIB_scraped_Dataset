      SER PROVIDES HYGIENIC MEALS TO MORE THAN 2.42 LAKH PEOPLE
      Posted On: 29 APR 2020 3:57PM by PIB Kolkata


    Kolkata, April 29, 2020


    Due to nation-wide lockdown in the wake of Covid-19 pandemic, South Eastern Railway (SER) has come forward with its social commitments. SER, till April 27, 2020 has distributed 2,42,813 free and hygienic meals to the destitute, poor and needy people nearby and outside the railway areas at Headquarters and in its four divisions viz. Kharagpur, Adra, Chakradharpur and Ranchi. COVID-19 has created an unprecedented crisis leaving a large section of poor people in starvation. The worst hit victims of lockdown are daily wage labourers, porters, rickshaw pullers, beggars, slum dwellers, homeless vagrants etc. including their family members. Out of 2,42,813 meals, 64,555 meals were served and distributed by Indian Railway Catering & Tourism Corporation (IRCTC) utilising its Base Kitchen infrastructure located in SER stations with the help of the Commercial Department. The rest 1,78,258 free meals were arranged by Security & other departments of SER. Various associations like South Eastern Railway Womenâ€™s Welfare Organisation (SERWWO), SER Bharat Scouts & Guides, St. John Ambulance Brigade, Civil Defence, etc also joined hands in this mass food distribution activities.


    Foods were distributed through the field units of SER over its jurisdiction and employees are also distributing foods at their personal level or jointly to the needy and poor people. This apart, SER employees are also distributing ration packets containing rice, pulses, potatoes, onions, edible oil, spices, biscuits etc.


    This distribution amongst the poor and needy people was done adopting adequate safety measures and social distancing and imparting best practices in this situation.
