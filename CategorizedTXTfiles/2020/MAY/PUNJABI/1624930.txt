      Economic packages under ‘Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyaan’ find support among educationists and farmers in the region
      Posted On: 18 MAY 2020 6:37PM by PIB Chandigarh


    Chandigarh, May 18, 2020


    Economic packages, as announced by the Finance Minister under ‘Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyaan’, find support among educationists, farmers and others in the region. With economy, infrastructure, system, vibrant demography and demand as its five pillars, Hon’ble Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi on 12th May 2020 put forth ‘Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyaan’.


    Finance Minister, Smt. Nirmala Sitharaman, proposed certain measures in relation to education sector in her latest announcements like PM eVIDYA, a programme for multi-mode access to digital/online education; Manodarpan, an initiative for psycho-social support for students, teachers and families for mental health and emotional well-being. Also, New National Curriculum and Pedagogical framework for school, early childhood and teachers will also be launched. To ensure that every child attains Learning levels and outcomes in grade 5 by 2025, National Foundational Literacy and Numeracy Mission will be launched by December 2020. Stating these initiatives as visionary, Dr Atima Sharma, Principal, KMV College, Jalandhar feels that it has given a significant direction to the educators. Mr. Robin Aggarwal, Director, Learning Paths School said e-content created under eVIDYA will help visually impaired and hearing impaired.

    The announcements under economic package propose to step upto Rs. 15,000 crores animal husbandry infrastructure development fund, with an aim to support private investment in dairy processing, value addition and cattle feed infrastructure. Incentives will be given for establishing plants for export of niche products. Narinder Rana, who runs a dairy, said this will help the dairy sector to flourish. Praveen Jain owner of a furniture manufacturing unit in Narnaul in Haryana is happy that work has resumed in his unit and said the economic package will bring a lot of relief to small manufactures.

    Gurdayal Singh, a farmer from Kaithal district in Haryana said the farmers are happy with the recently announced measures. As announced, NABARD will extend additional re-finance support of Rs 30,000 crore for meeting crop loan requirement of Rural Cooperative Banks and RRBs. This refinance will be front-loaded and available on tap. This is over and above Rs 90,000 crore that will be provided by NABARD to this sector in the normal course. This will benefit around 3 crore farmers, mostly small and marginal and it will meet their post-harvest Rabi and current Kharif requirements
