      Covering different sectors and sections, the economic packages under the “Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyaan” generate positive response in the region
      Posted On: 19 MAY 2020 5:19PM by PIB Chandigarh


    Chandigarh, May 19, 2020


    Covering different sectors and sections, the economic packages under the “Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyaan” have generated positive response among the stakeholders in the region. Recently, the Finance Minister, Smt. Nirmala Sitharaman has announced five tranches of economic packages.


    Jaswinder Singh, Sarpanch from village in Madanheri, Punjab while appreciating the increase in outlay under MGNREGA (Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act) said that more developmental works can be undertaken which will ensure more days of employment to the workers. The Government has proposed to allocate an additional Rs 40,000 crore under MGNREGA. It will help generate nearly 300 crore person days in total addressing need for more work including returning migrant workers in monsoon season as well.


    (Jaswinder Singh, Sarpanch)

    As announced by the Finance Minister, One Class One Channel initiative and addition of 200 books on e-pathshala would not only simplify learning for the children but would also ensure that they don’t face any problems as regards the syllabus, said Raghuraj Verma, a teacher from Punjab. Monica Sood, Principal, DAV Sr Sec School Hoshiarpur, Punjab expressed the same optimism on these initiatives.

    Jagmohan Singh Grewal, a farmer from Punjab said the recent announcements on agriculture marketing, fisheries sector etc. will be beneficial to the farmers of the country. The Government of India has announced Rs 20,000 crore for integrated, sustainable, inclusive development of marine and inland fisheries through Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY). For agricultural marketing, a Central law will be formulated to provide adequate choices to the farmer to sell their produce at remunerative price, barrier free Inter-State Trade and framework for e-trading of agriculture produce.


    (Jagmohan Singh Grewal, Punjab)

    On 12th May, 2020, Prime Minister of India Shri Narendra Modi gave a clarion call for “Aatma Nirbhar Bharat” to push the country towards self-reliance.
