      Ministry of Information & Broadcasting

      Financial assistance of Rs 34,800 crore received by around 39 Crore poor people under the Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Package (PMGKP)
      Posted On: 06 MAY 2020 5:01PM by PIB Chandigarh


    Chandigarh, May 6, 2020


    Using the digital payment infrastructure, around 39 crore poor people received financial assistance of Rs 34,800 crore under the Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Package (PMGKP) as on 5th May, 2020. The package was announced by Union Finance Minister Smt. Nirmala Sitharaman on 26th March, 2020 to protect the poor from the impact of the lockdown due to COVID-19. As part of the PMGKP, the Government announced free food grains and cash payment to women and poor senior citizens and farmers.


    While the Government is leaving no stone unturned to ensure that the relief measures reach the needy swiftly and in line with the intent of the lock down, the beneficiaries, under the package, in the region have expressed satisfaction with the services provided. Bina of Attawa village in Chandigarh, a beneficiary under the Ujjwala Yojna, said that earlier she received a free gas connection and now, a free gas cylinder is also being provided. Rajwana, from the same city expressed her gratitude to the Government of India for giving free LPG cylinder. While Hira, also from Chandigarh confirmed to receive a gas cylinder, said that the Government is extending all the necessary help in the light of situation due to COVID-19.

    Ravi from Kawwali village, Sonipat in Haryana thanked the Government of India for providing Rs 2000 to farmers under PM-KISAN and hoped that in future the Government would keep supporting the farmers. Neera Devi from Mandi in Himachal Pradesh appreciated the provision of free rations to the poor. She further said this has ensured that no family remains hungry during lockdown.

    As on 5th May,2020, Rs 16,394 crore front loaded towards payment of the first instalment of PM-KISAN to 8.19 crore beneficiaries. Total 5.09 crore Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) cylinders have been booked under this scheme so far and 4.82 crore PMUY free cylinders already delivered to beneficiaries.
